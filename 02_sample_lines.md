## stronger caption starters

Use these when a post needs more than a micro-line. Each one gives the brand a little room without slipping into brochure copy.

### texture starters

- This is the cookie for people who ask about the middle first, because the edge matters, but the center is what you remember.
- The cookie is thick because the middle needs somewhere to go, and that is where the fudgy part lives.
- If you are warming it up, keep it short because you want the center soft and the edge still holding.

### gifting starters

- Bring these when you want dessert to feel handled before you walk in the door.
- The pouches are matte deep berry, so you can hand them over without pretending you had time to wrap anything.
- A box of cookies says enough when the cookies are heavy and clearly from somewhere real.

### ingredient starters

- The green in Matcha + Macadamia is matcha, and the crunch is macadamia, which is exactly how that flavor should announce itself.
- Red Velvet gets the food coloring because red velvet has a job to do, and the rest of the menu gets its color from the flavor.
- These are cookies with butter and sugar, so the ingredient story is about taste and trust.

### local starters

- Irvine has cookie people, and they have been waiting for something with a soft middle and a little weight to it.
- Baked in a home kitchen in Irvine means the cookie comes from an actual person, and the pickup plan can still fit your Friday.
- The nice thing about local dessert is that it can feel like a find and still be close enough to grab before dinner.

### occasion starters

- Some Fridays need dinner, and some Fridays need tea with one cookie that feels like the whole plan.
- Send these to the friend who says she is fine, because the cookie can do the checking-in for you.
- When you remember dessert at lunch, you need something that looks considered and tastes like it was always the plan.

## flavor-specific lines

### chocolate chip

- The classic one, with a middle that stays soft.
- Chocolate pockets and crisp edge, the obvious first pick.
- For people who order chocolate chip to judge the whole place.

### triple chocolate

- Chocolate on chocolate, with the center doing the most.
- The one for people who meant it when they said dessert.
- Dark enough to feel serious and soft enough to disappear.

### matcha + macadamia

- The green is matcha, and the crunch is macadamia.
- Earthy middle, buttery nut crunch.
- For the matcha person in every group chat.

### red velvet

- Soft cocoa crumb, cream cheese on top.
- Red because red velvet should be red.
- The one that looks dramatic and eats soft.

### biscoff

- Cookie butter energy, thicker.
- Biscoff in the middle of a cookie that already had plans.
- Sweet and spiced, and very hard to split evenly.

### caramel + pretzel

- Soft center, salty snap.
- Caramel for the pull, pretzel for the edge.
- The salty-sweet one people pretend they bought for the table.

### peanut butter

- Peanut butter first, cookie second.
- Dense middle with the peanut butter doing real work.
- For the person who wants the flavor to show up early.

### apple

- Apple that tastes like apple instead of a candle.
- Soft middle with a little fall energy.
- The one that makes tea feel like a plan.

### almond

- Quiet and nutty, and very good with coffee.
- Almond in the cookie and clear in the bite.
- The grown-up one that still eats like dessert.

### raspberry

- The pink is raspberry.
- Jammy where it should be and soft where it counts.
- A fruit cookie for people who still want a cookie.
