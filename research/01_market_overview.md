# 01 Market Overview — OC Premium Cookie Market, Trends, Demographics

## Executive takeaways
Greta’s Bakies is entering a favorable but crowded “premium cookie” market: national cookie/category data point to growth driven by premiumization, flavor innovation, convenience, and indulgence, while Irvine/OC demographics strongly match the stated buyer profile. The most important local gap is that public sources do **not** publish a precise “Orange County premium cookie market size”; strategy should use national cookie/gourmet-cookie growth plus OC income/demographic concentration and competitor density as directional evidence, not as a hard TAM.

## Category growth and consumer trends
Multiple 2025 market sources indicate cookies are growing and premiumizing. Technavio/PRNewswire reports the cookies market is projected to grow by **$32.33B from 2025-2029** at a **7.3% CAGR**, with “rising emphasis on product premiumization” as a driver. Virtue Market Research estimates the **U.S. gourmet cookie market** at **$12.07B in 2024**, projected to reach **$18.63B by 2030**. IMARC estimates the global cookies market at **$40.62B in 2024**, projected to **$58.75B by 2033** at **4.19% CAGR**, while Straits Research estimates global growth from **$40.25B in 2024** to **$65.95B by 2033**. Snack Food & Wholesale Bakery’s 2025 “State of the Industry” emphasizes “flavor innovation and premium offerings,” directly relevant to Greta’s 10-flavor, Levain-style lineup.

Bakery-trend sources also support Greta’s product direction. Innova Market Insights reports that bakery trends include hybrid/indulgent creations and says **43% of consumers globally seek “crazy and indulgent creations.”** Barry Callebaut notes cookies remain a convenient, indulgent, affordable North American treat in 2025. Clean-label sources also matter: Innova’s clean-label trend report says leading demands include no artificial ingredients/additives and natural/organic sourcing, and Future Market Insights projects clean-label ingredients growing from **$57.3B in 2025** to **$212.4B in 2035**. This supports Greta’s “cleanest ingredients” story, while also requiring clear wording because the brand is **not certified organic**.

## Irvine/OC customer fit
Irvine is a strong fit for a $90K-$160K household-income premium-cookie buyer. U.S. Census QuickFacts reports Irvine median household income of **$136,719** in 2020-2024 dollars and per-capita income of **$64,055**. Data USA also reports Irvine median household income grew to **$136,719** in 2024, and Data Commons lists Irvine median age around **34** in 2024 — close to Greta’s target woman age 28-34. Irvine/OC also has many giftable-dessert occasions: dinner parties, offices, school/community events, bridal showers, and Friendsgiving/holiday gatherings.

## Local market structure
Public search results show many OC cookie competitors, from storefront chains (Chip Cookies in Irvine, Crumbl, Paderia) to home/cottage bakers (Dolce by Tina, Banh Box Bakery, decorated cookie sellers). Orange County Health Care Agency’s cottage-food page confirms Class A CFOs may sell direct-to-consumer and the OC cottage-food establishment PDF lists multiple Class A operations, proving the local cottage-food ecosystem is active.

## Implications for Greta’s
The best positioning lane is not “cheap cookie” or “viral color-cookie,” but: **small-batch Irvine pickup/delivery; thick 4oz cookies; clean-label indulgence; elegant gifting for OC hosts and offices.** The market rewards visually rich, shareable cookies, but Greta can differentiate through restraint: dense fudgy centers, natural-looking ingredient-led flavors, minimal coloring, and polished gift boxes.
